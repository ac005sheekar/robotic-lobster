I attended some ambitious workshops during my undergrad which included the direct affiliation of NASA and Microsoft.

Rocket Design Principle, NASA Solve-Young Scientist Meetup, City University, Dhaka, Bangladesh (2019)



The NASA Solve-Bangladesh Chapter arranged a "Young Scientist Meetup" at the City University in 2019. I attended consecutively two workshops in that event. They were: "Astronaut Workshop" and "Rocket Design Principle - Basic". My purpose behind doing those workshops were to find out the problems inside the ongoing space technology and solve them with some better technology. At that time, I was a core practitioner of Robotics. My intention was to find out the space related problems which I could solve with robotics.


Cloud Camp (Azure), Microsoft Student Partners, Jahangirnagar University (2019)



In 19 October 2019, I led my undergrad university team to attend the Cloud Camp (Azure) in Jahangirnagar University. That camp was arranged by the Microsoft Student Partners- Bangladesh Chapter. I was very enthusiastic to learn about the cloud architecture and networking components of Azure inside out. So I hopped into the camp and took 7 more junior students with me. We learnt about the Azure services for web and mobile application development, cloud operations, blockchain, Internet of Things (IoT) and Machine Learning ToolKits. It was a great time indeed!
